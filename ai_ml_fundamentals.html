What is AI?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) enables machines to perform tasks that normally require human intelligence—such as understanding language, recognizing images, or making decisions.

  • Chatbots that answer questions
  • Recommendation systems (movies, music)
  • Self-driving car systems
Slide 2 • What is AI?

Types of AI

  • Narrow AI: Designed for a specific task (e.g., Siri, Alexa)
  • General AI: Hypothetical future AI with human-like intelligence
Slide 3 • Types of AI

What is Machine Learning?

Machine Learning (ML) is a subset of AI that allows computers to learn patterns from data to make predictions or decisions without being explicitly programmed.

Example: ML models can predict trends, recognize images, or filter spam emails.

Slide 4 • What is Machine Learning?

Types of Machine Learning

  • Supervised Learning: Learns from labeled data
  • Unsupervised Learning: Finds patterns in unlabeled data
  • Reinforcement Learning: Learns through feedback and rewards
Slide 5 • Types of Machine Learning

How Machine Learning Works

  1. Collect and prepare data
  2. Train a model using algorithms
  3. Make predictions on new data
  4. Evaluate and improve performance

ML models improve accuracy through experience — the more data they learn from, the better their predictions become.

Real-world Applications

  • Healthcare: Diagnosis and patient monitoring
  • Education: Personalized learning tools
  • Entertainment: Recommendation systems
  • Finance: Fraud detection, automated trading

Key Takeaways

  • AI enables machines to act intelligently.
  • ML helps systems learn from data automatically.
  • AI and ML are shaping the future of technology and innovation.
